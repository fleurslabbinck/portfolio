About

This project was created as part of the group projects course at DAE, in collaboration with MyMachine Flanders. It began with a creative partnership between our team and an elementary school class of 8- and 9-year-olds, each of whom designed their own machine, ranging from house cars to happiness canons. Our task was to transform all their imaginative ideas into one cohesive game: Robotopia.

What I Learned

Robotopia was my first large-scale group project, where we worked closely together four days a week over the course of an entire semester. With only six people and dozens of dream machines to incorporate, communication quickly became one of the most important parts of our workflow. We had to rely on each other, stay aligned, and solve problems efficiently to keep things moving forward.

While the process wasn’t without its challenges, overcoming them taught me a lot about collaboration and strengthened my problem-solving skills. Everyone’s motivation made the project incredibly rewarding to work on. We entered the project as strangers, and left as teammates, friends and creators of something we were truly proud of.
