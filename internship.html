About

During my internship at IDLab-Media, a research group embedded within imec and Ghent University, I collaborated with another DAE student on a recovery-focused Virtual Reality football simulation project. The project was initiated by a PhD student from Revaki, Ghent University's revalidation and physiotherapy sciences group, to study how gamification impacts patient motivation and recovery effectiveness. An initial VR prototype already existed, but it prioritized motion data collection over immersion. We restarted the project from scratch with the goal of building a realistic and engaging football scenario designed to shift the player's focus from therapy to gameplay, and providing a strong foundation for future research.

What I Learned

The internship was an incredibly enriching experience where I acquired new skills and significantly improved existing ones. I learned to comfortably navigate my way through Unity, tackled complex NPC behavior, and explored animations and inverse kinematics. I led the development of a WPF tool, and familiarized myself with network communication, a topic not yet covered in the DAE curriculum. Additionally, working this closely with another student sharpened my communication skills. Finally, I learned to take initiative and work independently with a proactive mindset, taking the busy schedule of supervisors into account while still making the most of the available support.
