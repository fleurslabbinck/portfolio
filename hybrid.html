About

Throughout my DAE career, I had already come into contact with the concept of using multiple decision-making structures in one behavior implementation. All structures have their advantages and disadvantages, so the combination of different structures into a hybrid approach allows for combined benefits and lower chance of succumbing to the downfalls. This seemed like an interesting topic to dig deeper into for my graduation work. In the end, for my research topic, I compared a Finite State Machine Behavior Tree hybrid, or hybrid for short, to a pure Behavior Tree in an animal simulation Unreal Engine project. I performed tests based on the ease of implementation, the maintainability of the structure, and the performance of both the simulation and the decision-making structures themselves. Based on the results, and my experience with both structures, the hybrid is definitely a worthy contender to the pure Behavior Tree.
